What I'm learning from working in chartering so far

It has now been almost one month since I started my Werkstudent role in the Chartering Department at German Tanker Shipping.

Even in this short time, I've already gained a much clearer view of how fixtures come together, how information moves within the market and across the workplace, and how relationship-driven the commercial side of shipping really is.

What has stood out to me most is how much the job depends on timing, clear communication, and trust. From the outside, chartering can look purely commercial and numbers-driven. In reality, relationships and coordination seem to matter just as much.

So far, the experience has helped me improve my understanding of workflows, teamwork, office culture, and professional communication in a real shipping environment.

I'm still at the beginning, but the first month has already confirmed that this is a side of the industry I genuinely want to keep learning from.
